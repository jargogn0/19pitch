Published academic cost per hour for African low-resource speech collection. Hausa: $345/hr. We sit in the same band, with proprietary tooling that compresses delivery time. Source: arXiv 2507.17578 (2025) · arXiv 2510.12781 (RobotsMali, 2025)
Lever 01
Language scarcity
Swahili has tooling and contributors. Mandinka has neither. Rarer languages move toward the top of the band.
Lever 02
Recording conditions
Studio read-aloud is cheap to scale. Spontaneous field recording with environmental noise costs more.
Lever 03
Metadata depth
Audio + transcript is the floor. Speaker IDs, demographics, accent labels, and expert QA push the ceiling.
